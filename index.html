About Me

Hi, I'm Hıdır Arslan, a researcher passionate about cultural neuroscience, empathy, and cognitive science. My work explores how cultural contexts influence memory, empathy, and social interaction. I also focus on understanding how cognitive and neural mechanisms vary across different cultures, with a particular emphasis on metacognition and emotional regulation.

Research

My research spans across several areas of cognitive neuroscience, with a focus on cultural differences in cognitive processes, such as empathy, emotional regulation, and metacognition. Through the use of EEG and fMRI, I explore the neural correlates of these processes and how they shape behavior in diverse cultural settings. I also study the intersection of these topics with mental health, particularly ADHD and minority stress.
